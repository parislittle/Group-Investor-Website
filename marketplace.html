While this solution offers a way for consumers to protect themselves, it necessitates action from the consumers, requiring them purchase the item and carry it around with them rather than having the solution already embedded into the bottle.

        There are a few solutions in the market that modify the can itself, but none of them are marketed to prevent drink-spiking. Some companies, like Ball Packaging Europe, Foboha GmbH, and Can2close, use additional plastic components, which inhibits mass-production due to recycling difficulties of the combined materials. Other inventions, like the Xtra lid, the Soda Seal by the Ukrainian inventor, Johan De Broyer, and one done by Robert Davis use excess amounts of aluminum that are extremely costly when mass-produced. All of these lids are marketed as solutions to drink-spilling and preventing bugs and other external contaminants from entering the drink. Very few were brought to production and those that did failed to be successful in their launch due to reasons described above. There are no resealable cans to prevent drink-spiking on the market.

        Tabbed finds itself in a unique position as a startup in the hard-seltzer industry that differentiates itself with a drink-spiking prevention design. While the contents could be comparable to a brand like Truly, Tabbed's redesigned can lid that is seamlessly integrated into the product and positioning as a drink-spiking preventative measure provides consumers with an effortless and reliable way to protect their drinks from potential tampering.
